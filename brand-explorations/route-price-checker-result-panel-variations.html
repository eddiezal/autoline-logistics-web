Route Price Checker . Result panel variations

The form is standard (From ZIP / To ZIP / Vehicle / Submit) — borrowed from HeroRouteFinder. The conversion moment is the RESULT PANEL: what appears below the form after submit. All 4 variations have the comparison structure you picked (today + 30-day) baked in. Day-1 honesty: the 30-day column shows "Tracking" placeholders until the cron has 30 days of data. Same SD-live data used across all variations: LA → Austin, sedan range $900-$1,100.

Day-1 reality: Cron started writing Firestore snapshots 2026-06-14. 30-day comparison data ships ~2026-07-14. Until then, 30-day column shows "Tracking" placeholders. Once we have data, copy switches to real averages with delta arrows.

V1 . Side-by-side comparison

Two columns: TODAY (live SD data, green eyebrow) and 30-DAY AVERAGE (placeholder until we have data). Most explicit comparison frame. The "Tracking" placeholder honestly signals where the data will live. Transit + CTA in the footer. Best at-a-glance "are today's prices fair?" answer when 30-day data lands.

My take

V1 or V4. Both have explicit "Today / 30-day average" columns that respect the comparison structure you picked. V1 reads more like a card; V4 reads more like a data product. V4 wins slightly on future-proofing (adds delta arrow column without restructure).

V2 is the most ad-friendly today, but the comparison structure is the WHOLE POINT of the picker (vs the corridor pricing card, which is single-corridor). The chip placeholder undersells the future feature.

V3 is the most visually differentiated but adds rendering complexity (mobile bars look cramped) and the 30-day dashed overlay is harder to explain at small sizes.

My pick: V4 (Table). Information density, easy mobile collapse, explicit "Tracking" → "avg ± delta" upgrade path. The cleanest data product feel for a researcher who's comparing multiple routes back-to-back.
